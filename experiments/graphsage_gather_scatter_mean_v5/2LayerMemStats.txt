Created cs217-final-proj/experiments/graphsage_gather_scatter_mean_v5/ with an end-to-end 2-layer GraphSAGE forward:
run_torch.py: fully PyTorch end-to-end, including mean neighbor aggregation implemented with the same padded src_padded/mask_padded/inv_degree representation.
run_nki.py: replaces only the neighbor aggregation step (twice: layer1 and layer2) with your v1/Option-1 style NKI gather_mean_nki kernel; linear layers + ReLU remain PyTorch.
Both scripts build CPU tensors (src_padded/mask_padded/inv_degree) outside the profiled device execution; run_nki.py also computes a CPU reference for correctness to avoid polluting the NEFF.
Correctness
run_nki.py matched the full Torch reference with max abs diff ~7.45e-09.

Interpretation
After substituting the neighbor aggregation with your NKI kernel, the whole model becomes almost purely DMA-bound (NKI total_time ≈ dma_active_time), so further speedups will likely require improving the irregular gather/transfer efficiency (i.e., more coalescing / fewer transfers / better scheduling), not just reducing compute.
